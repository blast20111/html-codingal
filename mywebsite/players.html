what is volleyball?

Volleyball is a team sport where two teams, usually of six players each, are separated by a net. The main objective is to ground the ball on the opponent’s court and prevent it from touching your own

history of volleyball

🎈Invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in Massachusetts, USA

🎈Originally called “Mintonette”, it was designed as a less intense alternative to basketball.

🎈The name “volleyball” was adopted after people noticed the game involved volleying the ball back and forth.

🎈Became an Olympic sport in 1964 (Tokyo Olympics).
